# Product Principles

These guide every product decision at bndy.

## 1. Grassroots First

We serve the 200-cap venue before the 2000-cap venue. We surface the unsigned artist before the touring headliner. If a feature doesn't work for the smallest players, it doesn't ship.

## 2. Discovery Over Curation

We're not tastemakers - we're a discovery engine. The goal is to help users find *their* music, not tell them what's good. Personalization over editorial.

## 3. Data Quality is Product Quality

A gig with the wrong date or a duplicate venue degrades trust. Every user-facing feature depends on clean, accurate, canonical data. Invest in data integrity.

## 4. Community Builders are Force Multipliers

One engaged promoter brings 50 events. One verified venue brings credibility to a city. Design features that empower community builders first.

## 5. AI-Augmented, Human-Centred

AI does the heavy lifting - ingestion, matching, recommendations. But humans make the final call. Community verification > algorithmic confidence.

## 6. Local is the Product

bndy is only as good as its coverage in your city. A global platform with no local events is worthless. Depth in key cities beats shallow global coverage.

## 7. Real-World Outcomes

The metric that matters is: did you go to a gig? Everything else - clicks, follows, saves - is proxy. Optimise for real-world attendance.

## 8. No Dark Patterns

No manufactured urgency. No hidden fees. No engagement-bait. Music fans are skeptical of platforms - earn trust through transparency.

## Anti-Principles (What We Avoid)

- **Ticketing lock-in** - We don't sell tickets, we link to them
- **Pay-to-play** - Basic visibility should never require payment
- **Algorithmic opacity** - Users should understand why they see what they see
- **Engagement farming** - No notifications designed to create anxiety
